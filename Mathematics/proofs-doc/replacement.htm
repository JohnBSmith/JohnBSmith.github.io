Theorem. replacement
function f → A ⊆ dom f → set A → set (img f A)

The axiom of replacement, derived as a theorem from its prototypical form, the axiom of substitution. The premise A ⊆ dom f is actually not needed, as shown in replacement_strong.

Proof sketch
Consider the restriction f|A, which has domain A because A ⊆ dom f. Thus, according to the substitution axiom, its range rng(f|A) must be a set. But then, according to the subset axiom, the image f[A] must ultimately also be a set, because f[A] ⊆ rng(f|A). q.e.d.
